Card 1
Front: When did the Bhopal Gas Tragedy occur?
Back: Night of 2–3 December 1984.

Card 2
Front: Where did the Bhopal Gas Tragedy take place?
Back: Union Carbide India Limited (UCIL) pesticide plant, Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh.

Card 3
Front: Which toxic gas leaked during the Bhopal Gas Tragedy?
Back: Methyl Isocyanate (MIC).

Card 4
Front: Approximately how many people were killed immediately in the Bhopal Gas Tragedy?
Back: Around 3,000.

Card 5
Front: What is the estimated long-term death toll of the Bhopal Gas Tragedy?
Back: Over 15,000 deaths (due to long-term effects).

Card 6
Front: How many people were affected by the Bhopal Gas Tragedy?
Back: Over 5 lakh people.

Card 7
Front: What were some long-term health impacts of the Bhopal Gas leak?
Back: Respiratory problems, eye irritation, blindness, reproductive health issues, cancers, and genetic disorders.

Card 8
Front: Which U.S.-based company was the parent of Union Carbide India Limited (UCIL)?
Back: Union Carbide Corporation (UCC).

Card 9
Front: Who was the Chairman of Union Carbide Corporation at the time of the Bhopal Gas Tragedy?
Back: Warren Anderson.

Card 10
Front: Which Act was enacted in response to the Bhopal Gas Tragedy to deal with industrial disasters?
Back: The Environment Protection Act, 1986.

Card 11
Front: Which other legislation was passed for industrial safety after the tragedy?
Back: The Factories (Amendment) Act, 1987.

Card 12
Front: Which judicial principle was evolved in India as a result of the Bhopal Gas Tragedy?
Back: The principle of Absolute Liability.

Card 13
Front: Which Supreme Court case laid down the principle of Absolute Liability?
Back: M.C. Mehta v. Union of India (Oleum Gas Leak Case, 1986).

Card 14
Front: How is the principle of Absolute Liability different from Strict Liability?
Back: Absolute Liability does not allow exceptions/defences; industries engaged in hazardous activities are liable for harm regardless of precautions.

Card 15
Front: Which Act was passed to manage claims arising out of the Bhopal Gas Tragedy?
Back: The Bhopal Gas Leak Disaster (Processing of Claims) Act, 1985.

Card 16
Front: Which organisation was created under this Act to handle claims?
Back: Office of the Welfare Commissioner, Bhopal Gas Victims.

Card 17
Front: What compensation was paid by Union Carbide Corporation in 1989 as per Supreme Court settlement?
Back: USD 470 million.
